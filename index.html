    DIY inspection tips

    Regular garage door inspections are an important part of home maintenance that can help prevent costly repairs down the road. While hiring a professional to inspect your garage door is always a good idea, there are also some DIY inspection tips that you can follow to ensure that your garage door is in good working condition.

    One important tip is to visually inspect the exterior of your garage door for any signs of damage or wear and tear. Look for dents, rust, or chipped paint, as these can indicate potential issues with the functionality of your garage door. Additionally, check the weather stripping along the bottom of the door to make sure it is intact and providing a tight seal.

    Next, listen carefully to the sounds that your garage door makes when opening and closing. Any grinding or scraping noises could be a sign that there is something wrong with the tracks or rollers. If you notice any unusual sounds, it may be time to call in a professional for further inspection.

    You should also test the balance of your garage door by manually opening and closing it halfway. If the door does not stay in place when opened halfway, this could indicate a problem with the springs or cables and should be addressed promptly.

    Lastly, take a look at the safety features of your garage door, such as sensors that prevent the door from closing on objects or people. Make sure these sensors are functioning properly by testing them with an object like a broomstick before conducting any further tests.

    By following these DIY inspection tips on a regular basis, you can help ensure that your garage door remains in good working condition and avoid unexpected repairs in the future. Remember, if you ever have any doubts about the safety or functionality of your garage door, it's always best to consult with a professional for assistance.

    Common issues found during inspections

    Regular garage door inspections are crucial for ensuring the safety and functionality of your garage door. During these inspections, common issues may arise that need to be addressed promptly.

    One of the most common issues found during garage door inspections is worn out or damaged springs. Garage door springs are under a significant amount of tension and can wear out over time, leading to problems with opening and closing the door smoothly. It is important to have these springs replaced by a professional to avoid any accidents or further damage to the door.
    Bolingbrook
    Another common issue that inspectors often come across is misaligned tracks. If the tracks that guide the garage door rollers are not properly aligned, it can cause the door to become stuck or operate unevenly. This can put unnecessary strain on the garage door opener and lead to more serious issues down the line.

    Additionally, weatherstripping and seals around the garage door may deteriorate over time, allowing drafts, moisture, and pests to enter your garage. Inspectors will often check for any gaps or tears in these seals and recommend repairs or replacements as needed.

    Lastly, regular lubrication of moving parts such as hinges, rollers, and bearings is essential for keeping your garage door operating smoothly. Failure to do so can result in increased friction and wear on these components, leading to premature failure and costly repairs.

    By scheduling regular garage door inspections with a trained professional, you can catch these common issues early on and prevent them from escalating into larger problems. Taking care of your garage door now will save you time, money, and headaches in the long run.
