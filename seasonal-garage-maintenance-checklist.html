As the seasons change, it's important to make sure your garage is properly maintained to protect against the elements. One crucial step in this maintenance checklist is checking the weatherstripping on your garage door and replacing it if necessary.

Weatherstripping acts as a barrier between the outside elements and the inside of your garage, helping to keep out rain, snow, and cold air. Over time, weatherstripping can become damaged or worn down, which can lead to drafts and moisture seeping into your garage.

To check your weatherstripping, simply run your hand along the edges of your garage door. If you feel any gaps or cracks where air could possibly get through, it's time to replace the weatherstripping. You can find replacement weatherstripping at most hardware stores and it's a relatively simple DIY project.

By taking the time to check and replace your weatherstripping as needed, you'll not only be protecting your garage from potential damage but also ensuring that it stays comfortable and energy-efficient throughout the changing seasons. So add checking weatherstripping to your seasonal garage maintenance checklist today!

As the seasons change, it's important to make sure your garage door is properly maintained in order to avoid any unexpected issues. One of the key tasks on your seasonal garage maintenance checklist should be to inspect and lubricate the moving parts of your garage door.

Over time, the various components of your garage door can become worn down or damaged, which can lead to malfunctions or even complete failure. By regularly inspecting these moving parts, such as hinges, rollers, and springs, you can identify any issues before they escalate into larger problems.

Additionally, lubricating these moving parts is essential for ensuring smooth operation of your garage door. Without proper lubrication, friction can build up between the components, causing unnecessary wear and tear. By applying a high-quality lubricant to these parts on a regular basis, you can help prolong the life of your garage door and keep it running smoothly.

So next time you're tackling your seasonal garage maintenance checklist, don't forget to take some time to inspect and lubricate the moving parts of your garage door. It may seem like a small task, but it can make a big difference in preventing costly repairs down the road.
