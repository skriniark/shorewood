To prevent potential problems, take some time every few months to visually inspect the hardware on your garage door. Look for any signs of rust, cracks, or bending in the metal parts. Test the rollers by moving the door up and down – they should move freely without any resistance.

As a homeowner, one of the most important tasks you can do to maintain your garage door is to regularly lubricate its moving parts. This includes hinges, rollers, and tracks. By doing so, you can ensure that your garage door operates smoothly and efficiently.

Lubricating these moving parts helps reduce friction and wear, which can prolong the life of your garage door. It also helps prevent rust and corrosion from forming on metal components.

To lubricate these parts, simply apply a small amount of lubricant to each hinge, roller, and track. Be sure to use a silicone-based lubricant specifically designed for garage doors, as other types of lubricants may attract dirt and debris.

It's recommended that you lubricate these moving parts at least once a year, or more frequently if you notice any signs of wear or sticking. By taking this simple step in maintaining your garage door, you can avoid costly repairs and ensure that it continues to function properly for years to come.

DIY inspection tips

One important maintenance task that homeowners should regularly perform on their garage doors is checking and adjusting the tension on the springs. These springs are responsible for helping to lift and lower the heavy garage door, so it's crucial that they are in proper working condition.

To check the tension on the springs, start by disconnecting the automatic opener and manually lifting the door halfway open. If the door stays in place when you let go of it, then the tension is likely just right. However, if the door starts to fall or rise on its own, then this could indicate that the springs need adjustment.

Adjusting the tension on the springs can be a dangerous task, as they are under a significant amount of pressure. It's recommended to hire a professional garage door technician to handle this job to ensure safety and proper adjustment.

Regularly checking and adjusting the tension on your garage door springs can help prevent potential accidents or damage to your garage door system. By staying proactive with maintenance tasks like this one, you can help extend the lifespan of your garage door and keep it functioning smoothly for years to come.

One important task that homeowners should regularly do to maintain their garage doors is to replace the weatherstripping around the edges of the door. This simple yet effective maintenance step can help keep out drafts and prevent energy loss, ultimately saving money on heating and cooling bills.

Weatherstripping is a flexible material that is installed around the perimeter of the garage door to create a seal against outside elements such as rain, wind, and snow. Over time, weatherstripping can become worn out or damaged, allowing drafts to enter the garage and making it harder for your HVAC system to regulate indoor temperatures.

To replace weatherstripping, start by measuring the length of weatherstripping needed for each side of the garage door. Remove any existing weatherstripping by pulling it off or cutting it away with a utility knife. Clean the surface where the new weatherstripping will be applied to ensure proper adhesion.

Next, cut the new weatherstripping to size using scissors or a utility knife. Press the adhesive side firmly against the edges of the garage door, making sure there are no gaps or overlaps. Trim any excess material if necessary.

Regularly inspecting and replacing weatherstripping around your garage door is a simple yet effective way to improve energy efficiency and comfort in your home. By keeping drafts out, you can help maintain a consistent indoor temperature while also reducing energy costs. So don't forget this important maintenance task next time you're taking care of your garage door!
