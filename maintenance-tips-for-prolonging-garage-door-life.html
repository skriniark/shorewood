Signs that indicate a need for inspection

It's important to remember that your garage door is a hardworking part of your home that requires regular maintenance to keep it in top condition. One key aspect of this maintenance is testing the balance of the door periodically and adjusting it if necessary.

A properly balanced garage door not only operates smoothly and quietly, but also helps prevent unnecessary wear and tear on the system. To test the balance of your door, first disconnect the automatic opener by pulling the release cord. Then manually lift the door halfway open – it should stay in place without moving up or down. If it doesn't, this indicates that the springs may need adjusting.

Adjusting the balance of your garage door is best left to professionals, as working with springs can be dangerous if done incorrectly. A trained technician can ensure that your door is properly balanced, reducing strain on the system and prolonging its lifespan.

By taking simple steps like testing and adjusting the balance of your garage door regularly, you can help maintain its functionality and extend its life. Remember, a little preventative maintenance now can save you time and money on costly repairs down the road.

DIY inspection tips

One important maintenance tip for prolonging the life of your garage door is to regularly check and replace weatherstripping. Weatherstripping plays a crucial role in keeping out drafts and moisture, which can damage both the door itself and items stored inside the garage.

Over time, weatherstripping can become worn down or damaged, allowing cold air, water, and pests to enter the garage. This not only affects the temperature inside the garage but can also lead to increased energy costs as your HVAC system works harder to maintain a comfortable environment.

To prevent these issues, it's essential to inspect your garage door's weatherstripping regularly and replace it as needed.

It is recommended to lubricate the moving parts of your garage door at least twice a year, or more frequently if you notice any signs of wear or sticking.

What are some common signs that my garage door needs maintenance?

Some common signs that your garage door may need maintenance include unusual noises, slow opening/closing speeds, and visible wear on the springs or cables.

Can I perform maintenance on my garage door myself, or should I hire a professional?
